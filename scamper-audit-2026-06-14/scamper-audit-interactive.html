Does the AI Problem Finder lead you to the right problem?

Three L&D personas play the game — World 1 → World 2 → World 3 → The Challenges Wall — and stress-test whether the flow actually points you at a problem worth solving with AI.

14 June 2026 · Personas: Marit (Constrained Corporate) · Yael (Authentic Craft) · Daan (Stranded Pioneer)

The fixes, ranked — and how the personas voted

★ Consensus Triangulate the verdict

Proposed by Daan · all three agree
Marit ✓Yael ✓Daan ✓

Stop deriving the AI-fitness verdict from a single self-selected category. Cross-check category × root-cause text × recurrence, and flag contradictions (“you called this structural but described a people-won’t problem”).

World 3 · Step 1→2

★ Consensus Score before you select

Proposed by Daan · all three agree
Marit ✓Yael ✓Daan ✓

Run all 1–3 named challenges through the tier test and show their verdicts, then let the player choose the strongest AI candidate. Today selection happens before any criteria exist, so the abandoned challenges are never scored.

World 2 · Step 1

★ Consensus Re-frame the ending around capacity

Replace “the bit that’s repetitive, slow, or manual” with “what could you attempt that you couldn’t before — not faster, but at all?” One copy change; biggest alignment with the identity-transition thesis.

Full detail

Key tensions with no clean resolution

1. Defensible-and-small vs. real-and-new. Marit can only sell measurable efficiency upward; Yael says efficiency framing is the disease. The ending sides with Marit while claiming Yael’s mission.

2. Beginner legibility vs. expert respect. A worked example rescues Marit and repels Daan. The map currently serves the median, so it serves neither edge.

3. Selection vs. judgment as the core verb. Help you pick (Daan/Marit) or teach the judgment that makes a pick yours (Yael)? Currently a thin version of the first, none of the second.

Consensus points — fix these first

1. Don’t trust the self-click — triangulate the verdict.
2. Score before you select.
3. The ending betrays the thesis — reframe to capacity.
4. The Override Log / judgment move is missing from the game.
5. Context is decorative — make it gate the verdict.

Decision framework — priority order

Do first: Triangulate verdict (#1) · Score-then-select (#2) — these fix accuracy, the literal answer to “does it lead me to the right problem.”
Cheapest thesis win: Capacity-reframe ending (#3) — one line of copy.
Next: Live judgment beat (#4) · context-gated verdict (#5).
Defer: altitude switch (high effort) · worked example (low accuracy gain, make skippable).
