# AI Sandbox Personas

---

## 1️⃣ The Constrained Corporate Change Agent

### Core Context
Works inside enterprise constraints. High compliance, limited tool access (often Copilot-only), risk-averse culture.

### 🎯 Emotional JTBD
**"Help me feel in control and safe while navigating AI in a highly regulated environment."**

They want to feel:
- In charge of outputs (not blindly trusting AI)
- Protected from reputational or compliance harm
- Calm in front of ambiguity

They do manual validation rituals not because they love it — but because it restores their emotional safety.

### 👥 Social JTBD
**"Help me be seen as a credible, responsible, strategic professional — not an AI cowboy."**

They want to be perceived as:
- Serious and thoughtful
- Governance-aware
- Strategic in stakeholder rooms
- Modern — but not reckless

They care deeply about avoiding "AI-for-AI's-sake" optics.

### SUE Forces

**Pains**
- Red tape / unclear allowed use

**Gains**
- Faster analysis
- Stakeholder-ready narratives
- Structured thinking support

**Comforts**
- Manual review processes
- Established governance routines
- AI first mindset vs human first ("we need to do something with AI" without objectives)

**Anxieties**
- Compliance violations
- Bias in assessments
- Loss of professional credibility

### 🔑 What This Persona Needs from the AI Sandbox

The sandbox must:
- Include compliance-safe workflows
- Provide bias-check & validation templates
- Offer "Copilot-only" tracks
- Equip them with slides/templates to justify AI use internally

## 2️⃣ The Authentic Craft Professional

### Core Context
Strong professional identity. Values nuance, language, authenticity. Concerned about losing voice or becoming dependent.

### 🎯 Emotional JTBD
**"Help me stay authentic and human while becoming more efficient and effective."**

They want to feel:
- Proud of their output
- Like the work still reflects them
- Not over-reliant

When AI output feels generic or depersonalized, it triggers discomfort.

### 👥 Social JTBD
**"Help me be seen as a thoughtful, human-centered L&D professional — not a prompt jockey."**

They want:
- Respect for craftsmanship
- Recognition for nuance
- Not to look lazy or automated

They resist hype culture strongly.

### SUE Forces

**Pains**
- Time pressure
- Repetitive drafting work

**Gains**
- Idea expansion
- Structure support
- Language polishing

**Comforts**
- Personal writing process
- Creative ownership

**Anxieties**
- Loss of voice
- Cognitive dependency
- Generic, shallow outputs

### 🔑 What This Persona Needs from the AI Sandbox

The sandbox must:
- Teach voice-preserving prompting
- Include "AI as critic, not creator" workflows
- Build in reflection steps ("What's mine vs AI?")
- Normalize controlled use (avoid over-reliance)

If it becomes tool-showcase-driven, they disengage.

## 3️⃣ The Stranded Pioneer

*Built from qualitative research interviews with corporate L&D consultants.*

### Core Context
Personally ahead of their organisation. Already using AI daily — some have built complex automated workflows (chatbots, API integrations, automated reporting pipelines). But working in isolation: no peer to challenge their thinking, no organisational permission to scale what they've built personally, often constrained to Copilot at work while experimenting freely on a personal laptop. The researcher note on this profile: *"This is an identity problem."* They still define themselves as content creators. The shift to workflow designer hasn't happened yet — not because they lack capability, but because no one has given them the frame, the peer group, or the permission.

### 🎯 Emotional JTBD
**"Help me make the leap from personal AI tinkerer to someone who has actually redesigned how the work gets done — without losing my credibility or my organisation in the process."**

They want to feel:
- Ahead, not behind — they've invested significant personal time and they need it to be building toward something
- Like a pioneer with peers, not a renegade working alone
- In control of an increasingly complex personal AI practice that is starting to feel like an echo chamber
- That the identity shift — from content producer to strategic partner — is possible and has a path

They have a quiet anxiety about dependency: *"Het is ook wel een soort verslaving… dat je denkt zal ik eerst hem zijn vragen"* (It's a kind of addiction — always asking AI first). They're not sure where the line is.

### 👥 Social JTBD
**"Help me be seen as a strategic partner — not just a more efficient content producer."**

They want to be perceived as:
- Someone who redesigns systems, not just someone who makes slides faster
- Trustworthy to their organisation — not a renegade working around restrictions on a personal laptop
- Technically credible without being a developer: *"I'm not a coder or a developer… but I'm using systems that I never thought I'd use before"*
- Worth listening to in a stakeholder room — *"their respect for L&D goes up a bit, or at least respect for me goes up a bit"*

They are acutely aware that L&D faces an existential threat: *"I think that L and D is one of the biggest risks industries for AI… The train has already left the station."* This sharpens the urgency. They need to become the strategic partner before the role disappears.

### SUE Forces

**Pains**
- Working in organisational isolation — the "echo chamber" problem: no peer at the same level to challenge thinking or validate direction
- Red tape blocking what they've already proven works personally (Copilot-only restrictions while experimenting on personal laptop)
- Unclear budget and approval processes for experimentation — *"I'm curious but I don't have the time, space and tools to experiment"*
- Leadership resistance to moving beyond basic AI training — *"I don't want to pirate. I want to understand what I can do within constraints"*
- Content creation with AI disappoints: *"result was superficial 'fluff' with basic slogans and exercises"* — they've hit the ceiling of task-level AI use
- AI "goes down a rabbit hole" — frustrating iteration cycles after significant time investment

**Gains**
- Strategic partner status in stakeholder rooms — elevated credibility, not just efficiency
- Process-level AI integration that their organisation can see, trust, and scale
- Stakeholder-friendly dashboards: *"better conversations towards enhancement, continuous improvement"*
- Time savings redirected to genuinely higher-value work — not just faster versions of the same work
- A peer cohort operating at the same level — not beginners, not theorists

**Comforts**
- Manual validation layer — never direct copy-paste, always a review step: *"you need to look at it and go, really? That doesn't make sense"*
- Own content first, then AI as verification (*"eigen content eerst, dan AI als verificatie/check"*)
- Iterative improvement discipline — never accepting first AI output, always second and third passes
- Critical thinking as the quality control mechanism

**Anxieties**
- Being replaced — by AI itself or by colleagues who use it more fluently: *"Feel competent. Not feel replaced."*
- Loss of authenticity at scale: *"het is niet authentiek… als ik dat ga doen, dan heb ik het idee dat wat ik heb gemaakt niet meer voor mij is"* (if I do that, I feel like what I made is no longer mine)
- AI perpetuating debunked learning science (learning styles etc.) — undermines professional credibility if they use it uncritically
- Cognitive dependency developing without noticing: the addiction dynamic
- Compliance exposure from working around restrictions — *"I don't want to pirate"*
- The gap between where they are personally and where their organisation is becoming unbridgeable

### 🔑 What This Persona Needs from the AI Sandbox

The sandbox must:
- Provide a **peer cohort at the same level** — not a beginner cohort. This persona needs to stop working alone and find others who have crossed the personal-use threshold and are facing the same organisational ceiling
- Give them **workflow redesign frames**, not prompting tips — they already know how to prompt. What they need is a way to think about which processes to redesign and how to make that legible to their organisation
- Surface the **echo chamber problem** explicitly — the Override Log, and peer interrogation of override reasoning, directly addresses their isolation. They need someone to ask *"why did you make that decision?"* and mean it
- Help them **build the language** to bring their organisation along — slides, templates, stakeholder narratives that translate what they've built personally into something others can trust and approve
- Offer a **Copilot-compatible track** that doesn't make them feel like a second-class participant while also honouring what they've built on more capable tools
- Name the **identity shift** explicitly: from content producer to workflow designer to strategic partner. They are stuck at the threshold not for lack of skill but for lack of a frame and a community to cross it with

If the sandbox is a beginner space, they leave. If it's a tool showcase, they leave. What keeps them is a cohort of peers at the threshold — and a programme that names the leap they're trying to make.
