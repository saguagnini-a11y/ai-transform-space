## Consensus Points (All Four Voices)

1. **Judgment layer is the architecture's hinge** — it is under-designed and every other disagreement is downstream of whether Judgment does its job.
2. **Efficiency compressed to one session** — two weeks is convention, not conviction. This is the lowest-friction change.
3. **Pre-work Override Log before the Opening Expo** — seven days, one prompt, unframed. Converts participants from audience to analysts.
4. **Opening Expo arc reversed** — lead with struggle before success, not destination. Costs nothing. Changes everything about how the ceiling lands.
5. **Vocabulary change: rename layers** — "Surface Work / Standard Work / Original Work" replaces "Efficiency / Judgment / Capability." "Efficiency" is a management word that validates what participants came to move past.

## Biggest Fights

### 1. Protection vs. Disruption (Transformer vs. Pioneer — every lens)
The Transformer interpreted every design friction as a safety gap. The Pioneer interpreted every safety mechanism as a design compromise. Neither fully resolved. The Architect's reframe ("safety-vs-delay, not safety-vs-no-safety") was the most useful intervention.

### 2. Form vs. Rawness (Guardian vs. Pioneer on Closing Expo)
Pioneer: presentation format protects people from saying the actual thing.
Guardian: form is what makes difficult content transmittable. Raw exposure produces self-protection, not revelation.
Partially resolved: Pioneer conceded "curated, staged, read aloud" is a form.

### 3. Structural iteration vs. disruption assumption (Pioneer vs. Architect)
The Architect's solutions were consistently: tighter version of existing mechanism. The Pioneer's challenge: the architecture assumes good design produces identity transition. It doesn't — disruption requires a different kind of jolt. Unresolved.

---

## Sharpest Single Insight

> "The Override Log is load-bearing and may not be carrying the weight the architecture assumes."
> — Synthesis, S lens

This is the finding that everything else depends on. If the Override Log isn't functioning as a spine — if participants treat it as optional or episodic — the architecture's central mechanism isn't operating. Every other design decision is secondary to verifying this.

## Relevant Decision Frameworks

**Kaospilot 5E (Excitement → Entry → Engagement → Exit → Extension)**
- Pre-work Override Log strengthens Entry
- Expo narrative reversal strengthens Excitement without false aspiration
- Judgment critique format deepens Engagement
- Closing Expo as Override Log pipeline strengthens Exit + Extension

**SUE Influence Framework (behavioral personas)**
- Cautious Transformer = friction point at Entry and Engagement; requires visible safety structure, not hidden design
- Craft Guardian = friction point at Standards application; responds to explicit critique format
- Pioneer = pushes Extension; needs a designed outlet rather than diffuse "go further" framing

**Identity Transition Model (implicit in architecture)**
- The design assumes: Efficiency surfaces the ceiling → Judgment begins the identity work → Capability names the shift
- The council found: this works IF Judgment has sufficient friction. Without it, participants can complete the arc without ever entering identity territory.
