Unresolved Friction

Key Tensions

Protection vs. Disruption — Every Lens

The Transformer interpreted every design friction as a safety gap. The Pioneer interpreted every safety mechanism as a design compromise. The Architect's reframe was most useful: safety-vs-delay, not safety-vs-no-safety. Whether the architecture creates productive discomfort or just defers it remains unresolved.

Unresolved

Form vs. Rawness — Closing Expo

Pioneer: presentation format protects people from saying the actual thing. Guardian: form is what makes difficult content transmittable. Unmediated exposure produces self-protection, not revelation. Pioneer conceded "curated, staged, read aloud" is itself a form.

Partially Resolved

Structural Iteration vs. Disruption Assumption

The Architect's solutions are consistently tighter versions of existing mechanisms. The Pioneer's challenge: the architecture assumes good design produces identity transition — it doesn't. The identity shift requires disruption the architecture keeps smoothing over at the last moment.

Unresolved

Week 9 Identity Statement — Keep or Cut

Pioneer: a deliverable that makes people optimize for product rather than discovery. Transformer changed position in Round 2. The Closing Expo IS the identity statement — demonstrated, not declared.

Resolved — Eliminate (3/4 vote)

Round 2

Rebuttal Positions — Key Extracts

The Architect

Disagrees most with: Cautious Transformer

The Transformer's argument reduces to a single move across all seven lenses: protect the container. The anxiety in the first week of an Override Log is the work. You can build a container that holds difficulty without removing the difficulty. Safety-vs-delay and safety-vs-no-safety are not the same thing.

Concedes: the Judgment-to-Capability gap is real. The Transformer identified it correctly. The bridge needs designing, not dismissing as a Judgment sequencing problem.

Final Vote Compress Efficiency. Rename layers. Deepen Judgment. Pre-work Override Log. Guardian's Expo arc reversal. Accepts Judgment-to-Capability bridge as explicit design task.

The Cautious Transformer

Disagrees most with: Pioneer

The Pioneer's solution to every design problem is: remove the scaffolding. That's not bold design — it's designing for yourself. The Pioneer assumes the solution to underexploitation is designing for the comfortable. It isn't. If the Override Log is underexploited, the question is why — not who to remove the handrails for.

Changed on: Week 9 identity statement (Pioneer right — it optimises for product not discovery). Efficiency timeline (delay ≠ safety, conceded).

Final Vote One session Efficiency. Judgment as critique format. Override Log with cadence. Eliminate Week 9 statement. Do NOT start with Capability — the Expo comes first.

The Craft Guardian

Disagrees most with: Pioneer (Closing Expo)

Reading an Override Log aloud without form doesn't make it honest — it makes it exposed. Unmediated exposure in a public context produces self-protection, not revelation. The sonnet isn't less honest than raw feeling; it's what makes the feeling hold still long enough to mean something. Form is not abstraction. The Pioneer's "strip the format" move is the most seductive bad idea in Round 1.

Final Vote Pre-work Override Log (fully endorsed). Expo arc reversed. Judgment as structured critique. Closing Expo form built from Override Log, not on top of it. Compress Efficiency.

The Untethered Pioneer

Disagrees most with: Architect (iteration assumption)

The Architect's solutions are consistently the tighter version of existing mechanisms. But the architecture has an assumption baked in we haven't named: that participants will engage with the identity transition if the container is well-designed. I don't believe that. The identity transition requires a disruption the architecture keeps smoothing over at the last moment.

Concedes: form matters for the Closing Expo. "Read aloud" is not "unmediated" — the Guardian's critique is structurally correct. Closes with: test Capability-first with one future cohort.

Final Vote Pre-work Override Log. Expo arc reversed. Efficiency: 1 session. Judgment: critique format. Closing Expo form from Override Log. Week 9 statement: eliminate. Capability-first: test with one cohort.
Design Frameworks

How This Applies

Kaospilot 5E

Excitement: Expo arc reversal strengthens it without false aspiration.

Entry: Pre-work Override Log deepens it.

Engagement: Judgment critique format is the redesign target.

Exit + Extension: Closing Expo pipeline to next cohort.

SUE Influence Framework

Cautious Transformer: Friction at Entry. Needs visible safety, not hidden design.

Craft Guardian: Friction at Standards. Responds to explicit critique format.

Pioneer: Pushes Extension. Needs designed outlet, not diffuse encouragement.

Identity Transition Model

The design assumes: Efficiency surfaces ceiling → Judgment begins identity work → Capability names the shift.

Council finding: this works IF Judgment has sufficient friction. Without it, participants complete the arc without ever entering identity territory.
