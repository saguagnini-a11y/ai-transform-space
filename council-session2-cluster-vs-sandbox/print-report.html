Context

The Gap This Session Exposed

Session 1 ran the council without prototype knowledge. Session 2 was informed by a full review of the live codebase. The central finding: the prototype is the AI Sandbox (workflow redesign experience) and the architecture under review is the AI Cluster (identity transition experience). These are related but structurally different designs — and that gap is the real subject of this session.

Round 2 Rebuttals

Who Changed Their Mind — and Why

The Architect
Changed ✓
Disagrees most with: The Pioneer (Eliminate round — mandatory cycle)

Abandoned Week 5 Expo timing. Adopted the Transformer's reversed Opening Expo format and the Guardian's practitioner constraint (respond only with a specific override, no general encouragement). The format change matters more than the timing — a diagnostic conversation before the aspiration works in Week 1 or Week 5.

Did not change on the Closing Expo: World Café is wrong. Every participant reads one Override Log entry, five minutes, no audience. The awards section is cut. The name changes.

Adopted the Guardian's midpoint pin-up at Week 5 — a collective reading before Judgment begins changes the quality of peer sessions that follow.

The Cautious Transformer
Partial ✓
Disagrees most with: The Pioneer ("What became possible?" as opening prompt)

Adopted the reversed Opening Expo fully. Conceded that timing matters less than format once the format is right. Maintained Week 9 as a single facilitated session — sending people home to write identity statements alone produces polished output that isn't true.

Held the learning contract proposal but did not fully answer the Pioneer's challenge: a Week 1 claim may turn the program into a ten-week defence of that claim. Tension unresolved.

Named the learning contract as the clearest throughline: a claim that would be false now and true by Week 10, carried through the Override Log.

The Craft Guardian
Changed ✓
Disagrees most with: The Pioneer (Efficiency layer extended confrontation)

Adopted Transformer's reversed Opening Expo as sharper than own Round 1 Closing-to-Opening pipeline proposal (for Week 1). Maintained the pipeline as the right long-term mechanism from Cohort 2.

Added precision to the pin-up: what gets pinned is the override moment only — three lines: AI suggested X, I did Y, because Z. Anonymous. Read in fifteen minutes. Two effects: normalisation (everyone overrides something) and aspiration (range of what people override).

Extended Efficiency confrontation argument: the problem isn't duration, it's contrast. One week of efficiency work followed by naming what it can't do is more useful than two weeks of the same.

The Untethered Pioneer
Changed ✓
Disagrees most with: The Transformer (learning contract)

Replaced credentialing argument with the Guardian's pipeline — graduating cohort's Override Log entries opening next cohort's Expo. Pipeline stakes are higher than external credentialing because they're relational, not transactional.

Substituted the learning contract with a Week 1 question: "What do you most want to be wrong about regarding AI and your role?" A question generates humility; a claim generates a position to defend. This is the most original proposal of the session — and still unresolved against the Transformer's view.

Held the Efficiency confrontation position: requires structured output during two weeks so the rupture lands against actual investment.

Key Sharpest Insight
"The cycle design IS the first experiment. Participants who follow the default cycle are doing an experiment. Participants who design their own cycle are doing a different experiment. The Override Log captures both. The architecture doesn't have to choose."

Unresolved Tensions

What Still Needs a Decision

Learning contract vs. opening question

Transformer: name a claim at Week 1 (stake a personal commitment). Pioneer: name a question ("What do you most want to be wrong about?"). Claims defend; questions discover. Pioneer's argument — a Week 1 claim turns the program into a ten-week defence of an early position — went unanswered.

Unresolved — pilot needed

Efficiency layer duration

Guardian: surface ceiling fast, then contrast. Pioneer: two weeks of deliberate efficiency work followed by "Is this what you want?" — rupture requires attachment. Pioneer added implementation requirement: structured output during those two weeks. Guardian called this a facilitation failure, not a design proof.

Unresolved — pilot needed

"What became possible?" timing

Pioneer: opens sessions (aspiration before work). Transformer: closes sessions (evidence before reflection). Partially resolved — consensus: use as closing prompt from Week 4 onward, anchored to Override Log evidence from previous cycle.

Partially resolved

Opening Expo timing (Week 1 vs. Week 5)

Architect originally proposed Week 5. Conceded in Round 2 that format matters more than timing. Consensus landed on Week 1 with reversed format. Architect flagged Week 5 timing as worth a future pilot.

Resolved — Week 1, reversed format

How the Decisions Map

Kaospilot 5E

  • Excitement: Opening Expo reversed — ceiling named first, then exceeded
  • Entry: Learning contract or opening question (unresolved)
  • Engagement: Midpoint pin-up + Judgment as critique
  • Exit: Closing Expo from Override Log entries, every voice
  • Extension: Closing-to-Opening pipeline from Cohort 2

Identity Transition Model

The prototype proved the Sandbox produces workflow improvement. The Cluster needs to produce something it cannot: participants who can name what is specifically theirs — not what they're good at, not what AI can't do, but what the combination of their judgment and AI's capability makes possible that neither could alone. That answer lives in the Override Log. Every design decision should make the Override Log harder to fill in superficially.
