The Core Problem
Prototype (Sandbox) vs. Architecture (Cluster)

The prototype was built as a workflow-redesign experience. The Cluster is designed as an identity transition. These are not the same thing. Every REDESIGN verdict below is a place where the Cluster cannot inherit the prototype's design.

Agreed Actions
Key Design Decisions — Stated

These are the changes the council converged on across both rounds. The first four are immediate — no additional design work required. Items 5–7 require facilitation design. Item 8 is a one-cohort pilot.

1

Reverse the Opening Expo — participants present their ceiling first

Current prototype: Practitioners present new capabilities to a passive audience

Participants open the Expo with five minutes each: "Here is my current best use of AI. Here is what I can't yet do." Practitioners then respond — but only with a specific override moment from their own log. No general encouragement. The event becomes diagnostic before it becomes aspirational.

Implement now
2

Rebuild the Closing Expo from Override Log entries — rename it

Current prototype: World Café, before/after workflow showcase, awards, six rotations

Every participant reads one Override Log entry — the sharpest override moment in their log — and answers one question from the group. Five minutes each. Cohort of twelve = one hour, every voice in the room. No audience rotation. No awards. The name "Transformation Expo" signals a workflow showcase; rename it to signal what it actually is.

Implement now
3

Eliminate the awards section entirely

Current prototype: "Boldest Experiment," "Best Override," "Best Validation Discipline," "Most Transferable Prompt"

Awards individualise what should be a collective discovery moment. Competitive framing at the closing event is the wrong register. The Closing Expo is not a competition — it's a reading of what the cohort found. Cut all award categories.

Implement now
4

Name the Override Log as a professional portfolio artefact from Week 1

Current: Override Log treated as an in-program reflection tool only

Tell participants at Week 1 that the Override Log will become a professional evidence base — a record of judgment decisions that didn't exist before AI. "Here are twelve instances where I applied context, taste, and domain knowledge that AI couldn't supply." Naming this changes how participants write entries from the start. No structural change required — only a framing change in the Week 1 brief.

Implement now
5

Extend Capability to 3 weeks, compress Judgment to 2

Current: Judgment 3 weeks (Weeks 4–6), Capability 2 weeks (Weeks 7–8)

The hardest question — "What can you do now that wasn't possible before?" — currently has the least runway. Judgment at three weeks has room to drift. New allocation: Judgment Weeks 4–5, Capability Weeks 6–8. The reclaimed Judgment week moves to Capability, where identity work has to land.

Design required
6

Midpoint Override Log pin-up at Week 5

Current: Override Log is private — only surfaced at Closing Expo

At Week 5, every participant pins one override moment (three lines: AI suggested X, I did Y, because Z). Anonymous if needed. The cohort reads all entries in fifteen minutes. The collective reading does two things: normalises overriding (everyone is doing it) and shows the range of what people protect (aspiration). A private log for ten weeks wastes the architecture's most powerful comparative data.

Design required
7

Make the five-step cycle a default with a visible override mechanism

Current: Five-step cycle presented as the required structure

The cycle is not a procedure — it's one valid experiment design. Participants who follow it are doing an experiment. Participants who change it are doing a different experiment, and that design decision becomes their first Override Log entry. "I changed my cycle because the standard one wasn't producing the insight I needed." Make the override mechanism explicit and celebrated, not hidden.

Design required
8

Activate the Closing-to-Opening cohort pipeline from Cohort 2

Current: Each cohort is standalone — no connection to future cohorts

After Cohort 1 closes, three to four participants return for Cohort 2's Opening Expo. They present using the reversed format: ceiling at Week 1, what shifted in Judgment sessions, what they can now claim as theirs. Previous participants' Override Log entries are the most credible evidence that the transition is real — more credible than any external practitioner showcase. The standard-raising compounds across cohorts.

Unresolved Friction
What the Council Couldn't Settle

Learning contract vs. opening question — Week 1 commitment

Transformer: participants name a claim at Week 1 ("I will be able to say X at Week 10") and test it against their Override Log across the program. Gives a throughline. Pioneer: a Week 1 claim turns the program into a ten-week argument for a position staked before participants knew enough to stake anything. Participants filter Override Log entries toward the claim rather than following the log wherever it goes. Pioneer's substitute: "What do you most want to be wrong about?" — not a commitment but an admission of uncertainty.

Parties: Transformer vs. Pioneer · Transformer did not directly respond to the Pioneer's counterargument in Round 2

Unresolved

Efficiency layer as exposure vs. extended confrontation

Guardian: surface the ceiling fast, then provide contrast. Extended sameness produces exhaustion and resentment, not identity rupture. Pioneer: participants don't feel the limit of the efficiency frame if they're not in it long enough. Two weeks of deliberate efficiency work with real output — then "Is this what you want?" — requires attachment before rupture. The Pioneer's response to the Guardian's objection: facilitation failure, not design failure. Requires structured output during the two weeks.

Parties: Pioneer vs. Craft Guardian · Pioneer recommended a one-cohort pilot

Pilot recommended

"What became possible?" as opening vs. closing prompt

Pioneer proposed opening each session from Week 4 with the question, anchored to Override Log evidence. Transformer argued this asks participants to aspirate into a vacuum before the session has produced anything. The friction round produced a partial resolution: closing prompt, not opening aspiration, from Week 4 onward — but this was not fully adopted by all voices.

Parties: Pioneer vs. Transformer · Partial resolution in friction round

Partially resolved

Override Log fourth question — "What is uniquely mine?" vs. "What uniquely human knowledge did I apply?"

Architect: "What is uniquely mine?" is an identity claim. "What uniquely human knowledge did I apply?" is a process description. The Cluster needs the identity claim — scaffold the ramp to it, don't substitute it. Transformer: the Cluster's question is a Week 9 question being asked in Week 2. The four-field prototype form lands in the middle and stays there, but at least people can answer it.

Parties: Architect vs. Transformer · Architect's position held but ramp design remains unspecified
